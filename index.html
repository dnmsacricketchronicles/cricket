A Bit Of History

25th of June, 2022, was the day designated for holding the Inaugural T-20 Cricket Match between the President's XI and the Patron's XI, to be played under lights at the spectacular St. Peter's Open Stage and Arena, Boys' High School and College, Prayagraj (BHS).

Everything was set for a grand Inaugural event at the lush green cricket field. The members of the Association were divided into two teams, President's XI and Patron's XI. Team jerseys were provided to all the players. The teams were excited for their first outing under the umbrella of the Dhruva Narayana Memorial Sports Association, Prayagraj (DNMSA). A fruitful practice session was held on 23rd of June, 2022, which helped us shrug off our rustiness. The BHS ground staff had done a fabulous job in preparing a belter of a cricket pitch and a superb lightning quick outfield.

But.....
As they say, "Man Proposes, God Disposes!"

Everything was bright and sunny till 3.30 pm. Then the darkness and gloom started to descend over the city of Prayagraj as the dark clouds started to appear out of nowhere and started pouring! They poured and poured and poured! The rain would stop for a bit and then would pour again, playing hide and seek with our emotions! Soon, the excitement of the players turned into anxiety, looking towards the dark skies and praying for the rain to stop!

And stop, it did! At around 6.15 pm, about 45 minutes before the scheduled start of play, the Rain Gods showed some mercy! Unfortunately, by then the cricket ground had turned into a pond! It was impossible to play Cricket in those conditions. Swimming would have been another story but we were there for Cricket! Anxiety was fast turning into despair.

Resilience….

It was our inaugural event! We were not going to give up. The players were requested to gather at the ground by 6.30 pm. We had an impossible situation in our hands. We wanted to go ahead with the match, but it was not possible to play on the ground. It was getting dark as the sun had already set behind the dark clouds. We brainstormed a bit and came up with an alternative. Mr. David Luke, Principal, BHS, and Mr. Ravi Nandan, teacher BHS, suggested that we can go ahead with the match on the Basketball Court which can be readied for a shortened match in about half an hour! We took the offer as it was too good to refuse! The BHS staff immediately jumped into action and magically turned the Basketball Court into a Mini-Cricket Stadium!

After the customary inaugural ceremony, the Teams proceeded towards the make-shift Cricket Stadium!

President's XI won the toss and elected to bat first. The match was reduced to 7 overs aside. President's XI bludgeoned its way to an imposing total of 137 runs in their stipulated 7 overs, which, ultimately, proved too much for the Patron's XI who fell way short of the target and could reach only 76 runs. Their chase was wrecked by Anadi who took out Abhishek Bhushan and Prajwal, the main stays of Patron's XI, in the space of three deliveries.

President's XI had won the match in a dominant performance in all the three departments of the game. The winning team was handed over the winner's Trophy and the celebrations continued till mid-night!

Rain And Cricket Have A Long History!

Whether it's the 1992 World Cup Semi Final between South Africa and England, India vs England League Match in the 1999 World Cup, 2007 World Cup Final between Sri Lanka and Australia or the 2019 World Semi Final between India and New Zealand, numerous other International and IPL matches, all have been blessed by the Rain Gods! Even the 2024 T20 World Cup was not spared by the fury of Rain!

On any other day, I would put on José Feliciano's Listen to the Pouring Rain and would enjoy the weather! But not on a match day! Whether I am playing or India is playing or my favourite IPL team is playing, on a match day Rain is not welcomed!

Like Rain and Cricket, DNMSA too have a story going on! DNMSA's First Cricket Match was a rain-hit event in June 2022. When we decided to play our First Basketball Tournament in August 2022, Rain decided to visit us again! Rain greeted us on the inaugural ceremony of the Basketball Tournament with a fleeting special appearance but ensured a full-fledged presence in the final of the tournament leading to its postponement by a week and then again ensuring that the closing ceremony takes place indoors! The story repeated with rain interruptions in our First Football Tournament in September 2022.

Second T20 Annual Cricket Match of our Association was scheduled to be played on 30th June 2023. Yes! In that season when Rain enforces its supremacy over the other elements! There were two reasons for zeroing in on the month of June for the Annual Cricket event. First, the long summer vacations of the High Court and second, the desire to play under lights! Traditionally, Cricket is a winter sport in India. October onwards is the right time to play but the temptation to play under lights led us to hold the event in June. The evening temperature in October and later is not suitable for the elderly members of our Association who also wanted to be part of the game. Plus the threat of insects under lights during that weather! So, June it was! Under-lights it was! And, how can you forget – The Rain It Was!

We organized four very good practice sessions between 20th and 27th June and the weather was holding up very well! There was no sign of rain at all!

But can Rain stay away when DNMSA is organizing an event? Of course not! Rain ravaged the city for the next three days! The Annual Cricket Match was postponed….. indefinitely!

THE DECEMBER RAINS!

With rain hitting our plans once again, we had to make a few choices. We had to look beyond our desire to play under lights and decide on a different part of the year for our Annual Event, where rain would not be a threat anymore.

We made a choice! We chose winters to play the match. We decided that from now on, our Annual Match will be played either in December or January, and that we will give up on playing under lights.

So, the Second T-20 Tennis Ball Cricket Match was played on 23rd December 2023. President's XI won the toss and elected to bat. Put into bat, the President's XI put their foot on the accelerator pedal right from the first over.

It started to rain heavily. No, it wasn't the heavens opening up again, it was a downpour of fours and sixes from the bats of the President's XI batsmen.

Anadi coming one down started his innings with a six of the first ball he faced and then went on to hit three consecutive sixes of Mr. Anil Srivastava and one more 6 to follow off Srijan along with four fours across his innings. Ravi Nandan also smashed four consecutive sixes of Abhishek Bhushan. In their stipulated 20 overs, President's XI posted a daunting total of 281 runs. The score was helped by useful contributions from the entire batting unit, particularly by Justice B. K. Narayana's 39, Anadi's 53, and Ravi Nandan's 39.

In reply, Patron's XI were in the game till the 17th over, with Srijan (65), Abhishek Bhushan (52), Mehul (33), and Divyendu (47) keeping them in the hunt. President's XI ultimately prevailed over Patron's XI by 37 runs. Patron's XI ended their innings at 244 for the loss of nine wickets.

Anadi was adjudged Man of the Match (53 runs, 2 wickets, and 4 catches); Mehul and Srijan were given Best Bowler and Best Batsman awards, respectively.

The final scorecard does not reveal how close the match got at one point. Cricket was at its tremendous best that day!

THE THIRD EDITION

The Third Edition of the Annual T-20 Cricket Match was played on January 2025.

Patron's XI won the toss and opted to field. President's XI, buoyed by back-to-back victories in the previous two encounters, were delighted at the prospects of batting first on a tricky surface. This time, the match was enhanced to 25 overs aside instead of 20 overs.

President's XI's batting never got going. They struggled throughout and somehow managed to scrape through to 201. None of the batsmen got going. Justice B. K. Narayana (24) and Justice Ashok Bhushan (28) were the only notable contributions.

Patron's XI's innings started on a bombastic note with Justice Gautam Chaudhary smashing five consecutive sixes in the second over of the innings. At that point, everything looked over for President's XI.

But just about then, Justice B. K. Narayana bowled an incredible spell along with Anadi and got the team back into the game. Justice B. K. Narayana took 3 wickets for 4 runs and Anadi took 2 wickets for 25 runs in their four-over spells. At one point, Patron's XI were struggling at 78 for 7.

But once again, Srijan, riding his luck, scored 52 and, with able support from Divyendu (32), Aditya Chaudhary (28), and some extremely stubborn batting by Devrat and Samarth, secured a thrilling 1-wicket win over President's XI! More nails were chewed that day than starters were eaten!

Srijan was adjudged Man of the Match, with Justice B. K. Narayana bagging the Best Bowler award and Justice Gautam Chaudhary getting the Best Batsman award! Patron's XI lifted their first trophy.
With this win, the world of cricket saw the rise of a new rivalry—a classic one between President's XI and Patron's XI!
HENCE, THE NAME El Clásico.

El Clásico

On January 24, 2026, the teams played for the El Clásico Trophy for the first time. Patron's XI won the toss and choose to bowl first.

President's XI batted with authority and posted humongous score of 259. Saurabh Raj Srivastava top scored retiring on a score of 50 of just 16 balls. Vishnu score 48 and Justice B.K. Narayana, who opened the innings scored an unbeaten 29.

In reply to the gigantic total put up by the President's XI, the Patron's XI could manage only 174 in their stipulated 20 overs. Mr. Rajesh Pandey destroyed the Patron's XI's chase in the first over he bowled taking out the two most dangerous batsmen of the Patron's XI, Aditya Chaudhary and Divyendu Tripathi. After the twin setback, the Patron's XI could never really get going and were held back by excellent bowling spells by Anadi and Subrata who gave 17 runs in 3 overs and 7 runs 2 overs respectively.

The imposing total of 259 proved to be far too much for Patron's XI, ultimately losing the game by 85 runs.

Saurabh Raj Srivastava was adjudged the man of the match and Vishnu and Rajesh Pandey were given best batsman and best bowler awards, respectively.
President's XI lifted the El Clásico, their third Trophy!

Dawn Of A New Tradition

Ever since the inception of the El Clásico, the younger players of both the teams desired to compete against each other but without the Senior Legends. The younger players were eager to display their true talent and potential against each other which they could not do in El Clásico due to the applicability of special rules and restrictions on account of presence of senior members.

A decision was made. Beginning in 2026, a separate T20 cricket match would be played between the younger players of President's XI and Patron's XI two weeks after the conclusion of El Clásico. February 14, 2026 was chosen as the day on which the world would see the dawn of a new tradition. President's XI would take on Patron's XI in a twilight showdown! It will be a battle between the younger players where once they step out on the field, there will be no turning back! No additional rules that are applicable to El Clásico would apply. No stepping down after scoring a 50! No restrictions of El Clásico but still the same DNA! And they get to play under lights!

The rivalry of El Clásico gave birth to El Duelo

The Blockbuster Duel!

The stage was set on February 14, 2026, for the dawn of a fiercely competitive new tradition. Stripped of the senior legends and gentlemanly retirement rules, El Duelo commenced with President's XI, led by Anadi Krishna Narayana, taking on Patron's XI, captained by Mehul Khare. The rivalry was visually cemented as the two squads took the field, each sporting their own distinct team jerseys.

Patron's XI won the toss and elected to bat on a notoriously tricky surface. The pitch was even more unforgiving than the one encountered during the January El Clásico, perhaps reflecting the harsh extremes of Prayagraj winters—which have been known to plunge all the way down to zero degrees on a few occasions in history! The batsmen struggled to find their rhythm as the bowlers made merry. By the 14th over, Patron's XI was being suffocated at just 84 runs. Recognizing the crisis, Captain Mehul Khare took matters into his own hands. Unleashing a barrage of sixes left, right, and center, he smashed his way to an unbeaten 60, propelling his team to a formidable 164 in their 20 overs, even as Saurabh tore through the lineup with a spectacular 5-wicket haul.

The chase for President's XI began disastrously. Both openers were dismissed with almost nothing on the board. Manvendra and Saurabh steadied the ship, absorbing the pressure and taking calculated risks to keep the scoreboard ticking. However, the asking rate continued to climb on the deteriorating pitch, and in an attempt to accelerate, Manvendra perished. This brought Captain Anadi to the crease. He started cautiously while Saurabh tried to force the pace, but Saurabh soon fell as well. With 70 runs still needed off 54 balls, Vishnu arrived. He and Anadi navigated the pressure carefully until Vishnu departed after a crucial, gritty 22. Yash Tandon then joined his captain in the middle.

With 49 runs required off the last 30 balls, the 16th over proved to be the turning point. Facing Divyendu, Anadi launched a ferocious assault: a four, followed by back-to-back sixes, and a single to rotate the strike. Yash capped off the over by smashing the final delivery for another six. A massive 23 runs came off the over, reducing the equation to a seemingly comfortable 26 runs from 24 balls. But Patron's XI was not done yet. Devrat, Vinayak, and Prajwal delivered spirited, suffocating spells, claiming Yash's wicket against the run of play. Suddenly, President's XI found themselves needing a daunting 14 runs off the final over.

Aditya Chaudhary, who had bowled exceptionally well to choke the batsmen throughout the innings, was handed the ball for the ultimate test. Anadi, who hadn't faced Aditya so far in the match, was on strike.

THE GENESIS OF CRICKET

The Muir Cricket Club was established in 1970s and has played matches across decades and even two centuries! It got its name from the Muir Road on which the late Brij Narayana's (father of the late Dhruva Narayana) Bungalow, "VINDHYA" named after his late wife, was situated. The team was formed by Justice Bala Krishna Narayana during his school days and comprised of his friends and neighbours. Primarily, the team used to play its matches on the massive lawn in front of the Bungalow. Later on, the team was joined by Mr. Ananta Krishna Narayana, younger son of the late Dhruva Narayana and even further down the line, Mr. Anadi Krishna Narayana carried the legacy of the team during his school and university days in late 90s and early 2000s.

Muir Cricket Club has been now taken under the umbrella of DNMSA. The team will represent DNMSA in cricket matches against external teams.

Carrying forward the long-cherished family tradition of leading the Muir Cricket Club, Yuvaan Krishna Narayana, great-grandson of the late Dhruva Narayana, together with his family and friends, is poised to guide the Club towards even greater success in the years ahead.

Established in 1970s and still going strong in 2026, Muir Cricket Club implanted the DNA of cricket in the Narayana family and also in their friends!
